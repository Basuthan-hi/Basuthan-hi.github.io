Introduction

Tissues are groups of similar cells working together to perform specific functions. In animals, they include epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissues

History

Xavier Bichat introduced the word tissue into the study of anatomy by 1801.He was "the first to propose that tissue is a central element in human anatomy, and he considered organs as collections of often disparate tissues, rather than as entities in themselves".

Epithelial Tissues

It Covers and protects body surfaces, and Involved in nutrient absorption and hormone secretion.

Connective Tissues

They help maintain body shape and structure, and provide support to muscles and nerves.

Muscle Tissues

They provide force and movement, and they are involved in muscle contraction and relaxation.

Nervous Tissues

Contains neurons for transmitting impulses and neuroglia for support. Found in the brain, spinal cord (CNS)
